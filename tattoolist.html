The Challenge

In our society there is more and more the wish to achieve life goals or memories with a unique tattoo.
Now it is to discover how tattoo fans have done their tattoo research so far…

01. Understanding the user

The challenge
  • My method was to clearly identify the challenges of the problem on the basis of an initial „market analysis“ and „user interviews“.
  • With the results of the observations I easily defined the „persona“, „user journey“ and „user flow“.
  • The challenge was to structure the first generic terms by „cardsorting“ and a defined „sitemap“.
  • Only a repeated modification of the „sitemap“ hierarchy could be used as a basis for the first „mockups“.

    • I believe by designing a collage feature the tattoo fan can brief his tattoo preferences to the artist in a more meaningful way.

    Competetive analysis

    Before I start with personal interviews, it is important to consider the following on the list of Business requirements:

    • Which target group is important for me?
    • What are the weaknesses of established competitors?
    • What chances do I get from this?

    Goals
    • Find out how users research tattoos.
    • Find the biggest challenges when deciding on a tattoo.
    • Find out which characteristics are important for a final booking.
    Result
    • rating & user feedback
    • booking process
    • research on only one social channel
    Proto persona

    The main differences in the definition of the two proto personas were:

    • Research habits (youtube, facebook, bookcovers, google)
    • Motivation
    • Frustration (big confusing tattoo libraries)
    • User journey

    I analysed the ups & downs of the booking by following the user journey of the tattoo fans. Afterwards new opportunities for the user flow were created.

    Result

    The other group is looking for an artist with the specific tattoostyle.

    02. Define the user experience

    Cardsorting to improve the sitemap

    „We let ourselves be guided by the outside, but our navigation lies within“ – Karl Talnop

    The intuitive way is the best. As a result of the the card sorting exercise the most choosen categories were:

    • Tattoos with tattoo style, styles, artists & styles
    • Studio with studios & artists, locations, studios

    Hypothese Statement

    We believe by designing a collage feature the tattoo fan can brief his tattoo preferences to the artist in a more meaningful way.

    The challenge

    The clear black gray focus in the sketch of „low-fidelity wireframes“ helped to solve the major challenge of a clear „user flow“ through the application.

    While sketching I always kept the key aspects of the user journey and the persona in mind:

    • Trustful processes
    • Fast processes
    • Simple forwarding

    03. Usability test

    Rainbow spread sheet

    All sessions were recorded via skype and AZ Screen Recorder. The insights were listed in the usability report and frequency rated via rainbow spread sheet.

    Degree of severity

    The observations were sorted and handled according to their severity. Thanks to the feedback, the content hierarchy of the cards and the calendar was improved.

    04. Design the prototype

    The final challenge was to reflect the key solution elements in a clear presentation. In the future, the typical structure of presentations and style guieds will be more internalized.

    The original idea of an extensive collection of data has been given a more personal touch during the implementation. The artist portraits have user-oriented data for matching, as well as a simplified template for contact exchange via forms and collages between artist and user
