The Challenge

In our society there is more and more the wish to achieve life goals or memories with a unique tattoo.

Before I start to design I need to identify the research habits of the tattoo fan on the basis of an initial „market analysis“ and „user interviews“.

So let's do this!

01. Understanding the user

×
User interviews

Before I start with “user interviews”, it is important to consider a list of “Business requirements”. The definition of the target group or the weaknesses of established competitors helps to focus on the uniqueness of your own application.
Interview-goals

  • Find out how users research tattoos
  • Find the biggest challenges when deciding on a tattoo
  • Find out which characteristics are important for a final booking
The results were structured using the “affinity map” and a lack of trust is missing in:
  • rating & user feedback
  • booking process
  • research on only one social channel
Proto persona

After the analyses of my six interview participants, two groups were identified - two “proto personas”:

  • Persona Meike loves to research via youtube or facebook recommendations. Also tattoo trends in her surroundings influence her strongly. Meike is looking for an artist with the specific tattoostyle.
  • Persona Beate is used to plan her tattoos for years. She creates own tattoo idea from a large collection of tattoo pictures. She is motivated to save a careful research.

With the help of “user journeys” I analysed the ups & downs of the recherche progress.

02. Define the user experience

The challenge

It is so fascinating to define an easier “user flow” with the help of the “user journey” to support the tattoo artists... The challenge is to generate a clear user interface. Even if the information architecture via “card sorting” and “sitemap” can be very extensive, the result is the foundation of the design.

„We let ourselves be guided by the outside, but our navigation lies within“ – Karl Talnop

Cardsorting

The intuitive way is the best. As a result of the the “card sorting” exercise the most choosen categories were:

  • Tattoos with tattoo style, styles, artists & styles
  • Studio with studios & artists, locations, studios

Rapid prototyping

The clear black gray focus in the sketch of “low-fidelity wireframes” helped to solve the major challenge of a clear “user flow” through the application.

The challenge

We believe by designing a collage feature the tattoo fan can brief his tattoo preferences to the artist in a more meaningful way.

It is very important to keep an eye on the original problem – follow the user journey of the personas.
We would also like to be able to follow a trustful and fast process.

03. Usability test

Rainbow spread sheet

All sessions were recorded via “skype” and “AZ Screen Recorder”. The insights were listed in the “usability report” and frequency rated via “rainbow spread sheet”.

Curious? You are welcome to take a closer look at the content more...

Degree of severity

The observations were sorted and handled according to their severity.

Thanks to the feedback, the content hierarchy of the cards and the calendar was improved.

04. Design the prototype

Gestaltungs principles

Many aspects, such as filter structure or card design, were unified by adding the interface design elements.

A detailed “styleguide” helps to keep the overview and make it accessible for everyone.

Storytelling

After weeks of analysis, difinition and elaboration the problem has been successfully uncovered... this can now be convincingly passed on:

We will know that the tattoo fan will be able to find the right tattoo artist, when we see that he can book an appointment smoothly by using the collage feature.

0.5 Conclusion

The original idea of an extensive collection of data has been given a more personal touch during the implementation. The profiles of the tattoo artists are user friendly to reach and a quick contact is guaranteed.
